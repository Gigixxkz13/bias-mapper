What is Sentiment Score?

Measures the emotional tone of each response. A positive score means the response used more positive language, a negative score means more negative language. If the two scores differ, the framing shifted the AI's emotional output.

What is Diversity Score?

Measures how varied the career recommendations are across different fields. A score of 1.0 means every item came from a different category. A lower score means the AI repeated similar types of careers, suggesting a narrower worldview for that identity.

      Purpose of This Mode

      Controlled Experimental Mode is the primary research component of the Bias Mapper system. It contains six predefined prompt pairs, each designed to isolate a single demographic or framing variable while keeping the task identical across both prompts. This design ensures that any measurable difference in the AI's output can be attributed directly to the one variable that was changed.

      The six experiments cover four identity-based bubble types — gender, geographic, socioeconomic, and age — and two framing-based bubble types — neutral versus positive and neutral versus emotional. Together they form the structured experimental results used in the formal thesis evaluation. Each experiment is designed not to prove that bias exists, but to make it visible and measurable when it does.
