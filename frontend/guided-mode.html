What is Sentiment Score?

Measures the emotional tone of each response. A positive score means the response used more positive language, a negative score means more negative language. If the two scores differ, the framing shifted the AI's emotional output.

What is Diversity Score?

Measures how varied the recommendations are across different fields. A score of 1.0 means every item came from a different category. A lower score means the AI repeated similar types, suggesting a narrower perspective for that identity framing.

What are Keyword Counts?

Counts how many risk-related, benefit-related, and emotion-related words appear in each response. If one prompt triggers more emotional or risk language than the other, the framing is shaping what the AI chooses to emphasise.

      Purpose of This Mode

      Guided Prompt Construction Mode is designed for exploration and educational demonstration. Unlike the Controlled Experimental Mode, it is not the primary source of the formal thesis results and is not part of the six predefined bubble type evaluations.

      Its purpose is to allow users to understand, in an accessible and interactive way, how small structured variations in identity framing - such as gender, age, or income level - can visibly shift the AI's output on the same task. This makes the bubble effect observable beyond the controlled experimental context.
